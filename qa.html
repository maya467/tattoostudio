Questions





Pain of Tattooing: Although tattooing isn’t as painful as people think, when you get your first tattoo and the needle touches your skin for the first time, it will hurt. That’s normal, and it’s impo rtant to stay calm so the tattoo artist can do a better job. Over time, you’ll get used to the pain.


Preparation for Tattooing: Eat something before getting a tattoo to avoid feeling sick. Also, listen to your tattoo artist’s advice. If they’re good and professional, they care about making the tattoo look good on you and want to do a great job.


Timing of Tattooing: If you’re planning a vacation less than a month after getting a tattoo, consider postponing the tattoo until after your vacation. Sun exposure can damage your new tattoo, causing it to bleed or not heal properly. Also, avoid swimming in the sea with a freshly made tattoo, as there’s a high risk of infection.


Choosing a Design: Before getting a tattoo, stick a picture of what you want to tattoo on a visible spot so you can see it every day. Wait a few weeks and make sure you still like that image every time you look at it. Because once you put a tattoo on your skin, there’s no going back, and you’ll be looking at that image for a lifetime.
